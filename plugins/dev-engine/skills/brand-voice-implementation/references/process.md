# Brand Voice Implementation — Detailed Reference

## Formality Spectrum

| Level | Use For | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Formal | Legal documents, investor comms, crisis responses | "The company is committed to resolving this matter." |
| Professional | B2B content, whitepapers, case studies | "Our platform helps teams reduce deployment time by 40%." |
| Conversational | Blog posts, social media, email newsletters | "Ever spent hours debugging a deployment? We fixed that." |
| Casual | Community engagement, behind-the-scenes | "Quick update from the team — we just shipped something cool." |

## Tone Attributes (pick 3-5)

| Attribute | What It Sounds Like | Best For |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Authoritative | Confident, definitive statements | B2B, enterprise, technical products |
| Friendly | Approachable, warm, supportive | Consumer products, communities |
| Trustworthy | Reliable, transparent, honest | Finance, healthcare, security |
| Educational | Informative, clear, helpful | SaaS, developer tools, platforms |
| Inspiring | Motivational, empowering | Lifestyle brands, coaching |
| Innovative | Forward-thinking, bold | Tech, startups |
| Witty | Clever, entertaining (use sparingly) | Consumer brands with personality |

## Personality Archetypes

### The Expert
- Tone: Knowledgeable, confident, informative
- Content: Data-driven, research-backed, educational
- Example: "Our research shows that 87% of businesses..."
- Best for: B2B SaaS, enterprise, developer tools

### The Friend
- Tone: Warm, supportive, conversational
- Content: Relatable, helpful, encouraging
- Example: "We get it — marketing can be overwhelming..."
- Best for: Consumer products, small business tools

### The Innovator
- Tone: Visionary, bold, forward-thinking
- Content: Trend-setting, disruptive perspectives
- Example: "The way teams work is fundamentally changing..."
- Best for: Tech startups, platforms

### The Guide
- Tone: Wise, patient, instructive
- Content: Step-by-step, clear, actionable
- Example: "Let's walk through this together..."
- Best for: Education, onboarding-heavy products

### The Motivator
- Tone: Energetic, positive, inspiring
- Content: Empowering, action-oriented, transformative
- Example: "You have the power to transform your business..."
- Best for: Fitness, coaching, personal development

## Persona-to-Tone Mapping

### Decision Makers (C-Suite)
- Tone: Professional, strategic, ROI-focused
- Content: High-level insights, business impact, competitive advantages
- Pain points: Growth, efficiency, competition
- Format: Executive summaries, one-pagers, metrics-first

### Practitioners (Managers / ICs)
- Tone: Practical, supportive, educational
- Content: How-to guides, best practices, tools
- Pain points: Time, resources, skills
- Format: Step-by-step guides, checklists, tutorials

### Innovators (Early Adopters)
- Tone: Exciting, forward-looking, visionary
- Content: Trends, new features, future predictions
- Pain points: Staying ahead, differentiation
- Format: Deep dives, beta access, technical previews

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Website Copy
- Headlines: 6-12 words, benefit-focused
- Body: Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- CTAs: Action-oriented, specific ("Start free trial" not "Learn more")

### Email Marketing
- Subject lines: 30-50 characters, personalized
- Preview text: Complement subject, add urgency/curiosity
- Body: Scannable, one main message per email

### Blog Content
- Title: Include primary keyword, under 60 characters
- Introduction: Hook within first 50 words
- Sections: 200-300 words each
- Lists: 5-7 items optimal

### Social Media
- LinkedIn: Professional insights, thought leadership
- Twitter/X: Quick tips, engaging questions, thread stories
- Instagram: Visual storytelling, behind-the-scenes
- All: Adapt length and tone to platform norms

## Language Rules

### Words to Use
- Action verbs: Build, ship, launch, grow, simplify, accelerate
- Positive descriptors: Clear, fast, reliable, intuitive
- Outcome-focused: Results, growth, impact, savings

### Words to Avoid
| Category | Banned Words | Use Instead |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Weak intensifiers | Very, really, extremely, incredibly | (remove or use specific data) |
| Filler | Just, actually, basically, in order to | (remove) |
| Jargon | Synergy, leverage, bandwidth, paradigm | Collaboration, use, capacity, approach |
| Overused | Innovative, disruptive, cutting-edge | New, different, modern |
| Hedging | Maybe, hopefully, we think | (be direct or remove) |
| Negative | Can't, won't, impossible, problem | Alternative, challenge, opportunity |

## Grammar and Mechanics

### Punctuation
- Oxford comma: Always ("speed, reliability, and simplicity")
- Em dashes: For emphasis ("The answer — surprisingly — was simple")
- Exclamation marks: Maximum one per content piece
- Periods: In complete sentences and help text, not in short labels

### Capitalization
- H1/page titles: Title Case
- H2-H6: Sentence case
- Product names: As trademarked
- Job titles: Lowercase unless before a name
- Buttons and labels: Sentence case

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Numerals for 10 and above
- Always numerals for percentages, measurements, money
- Use commas in thousands (1,000 not 1000)

## Inclusivity Guidelines

- Use gender-neutral language (they/them, "team" not "guys")
- Avoid idioms that don't translate globally
- Consider cultural differences in humor and references
- Ensure content is accessible (alt text, heading structure, contrast)
- Represent diverse perspectives in examples and imagery

## Brand Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any content:
- [ ] Matches declared brand voice and formality level
- [ ] Tone attributes consistent throughout
- [ ] Free of banned words and jargon
- [ ] "You/your" used more than "we/our"
- [ ] Active voice used 80%+ of the time
- [ ] Benefits stated before features
- [ ] Appropriate CTA for the context
- [ ] Grammar rules followed (Oxford comma, capitalization, numbers)
- [ ] Inclusive and accessible
- [ ] Proofread by someone other than the author
